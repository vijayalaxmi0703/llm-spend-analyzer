# Go-to-Market Strategy

## Founder personas
- Early-stage technical founder managing AI and engineering spend.
- Head of Ops at a pre-seed startup with multiple AI subscriptions.
- Finance lead responsible for SaaS budgeting and runway.

## Distribution channels
- Product Hunt launch with a strong founder-focused narrative.
- Twitter/X threads showing before/after AI spend examples.
- Hacker News and Indie Hackers posts about startup cost optimization.
- Founder communities like YC Startup School, SaaS Slack, and SaaHacks.

## Community strategy
- Share weekly AI spend audit tips in r/startups, r/SaaS, r/artificial, and r/EntrepreneurRideAlong.
- Post case studies in YC founder groups and relevant LinkedIn circles.
- Seed the first users through founder Slack communities like On Deck, Indie Hackers, and SaaS Club.

## Product Hunt plan
- Launch with a strong headline: “Credex Audit — AI spend audits for founders.”
- Highlight value props: no login, no hidden fees, shareable reports.
- Offer a launch narrative: built during an internship sprint for SaaS founders.

## First 100 users
- Reach out to founders in early-stage communities with a private audit link.
- Offer a follow-up optimization session for teams with > $500/month savings.
- Collect usage feedback from the first 10 founders and iterate the recommendation logic.

## Traction estimates
- Week 1: 300 visits from Product Hunt and Twitter, 40 audits, 10 leads.
- Month 1: 1,200 visits, 150 audits, 35 warm leads.
- Focus on conversion from audit result to email capture by keeping the gate after value.
